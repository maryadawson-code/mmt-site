Mary Womack

Federal Civilian Account Lead

Rocket Data

Mission. Technology. Transformation.

I help leaders navigate the complexity of federal health IT. Through my newsletter and podcast, I translate policy into actionable intelligence for government contractors, defense stakeholders, and decision-makers.

My focus areas include the Defense Health Agency (DHA), Veterans Affairs (VA), Military Health System (MHS) modernization, data governance, AI implementation, and federal acquisition processes.

Every analysis I produce comes back to one question: "Does this save lives or enhance readiness?"

Background

I've spent my career at the intersection of health strategy and technology, working with federal agencies and commercial partners to modernize healthcare delivery. My experience spans program management, business development, and strategic communications in the defense health space.

Previously, I served at Leidos supporting federal health IT programs. Today, as Federal Civilian Account Lead at Rocket Data, I continue to bring commercial best practices to government while maintaining a focus on what actually moves the mission forward.

What I Believe

The federal health IT space is full of talking points and innovation theater. I believe in cutting through the noise to focus on what works: practical solutions, credible analysis, and unfiltered perspectives on the policies and technologies shaping military medicine.

About Mission Meets Tech

Mission Meets Tech is my platform for federal health IT intelligence. The newsletter reaches 1,191+ subscribers—59.4% of whom are CXO or director level—concentrated in the DC-Baltimore corridor.

The brand represents what I believe matters most in this space: the mission comes first, technology enables it, and transformation happens when both align.

Fed UP: Where Mission Meets Reality is the podcast I co-host with Sara Byrd. We dive deep into the policy fights, technology bets, and leadership decisions that are actually shaping federal health IT—not just the talking points.
