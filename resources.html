Source Hierarchy (Non-Negotiable)

When conducting defense appropriations research, source authority matters. Use this hierarchy to resolve conflicts and establish credibility.

CRITICAL: Authorization (NDAA) ≠ Appropriation. Never treat NDAA dollars as spending authority.

Priority Source Type What It Contains
1 Enacted/Conference text + Joint Explanatory Statement (JES) Final law + authoritative intent language
2 House/Senate versions + Committee Reports Chamber positions, report language, directives
3 Prior-year enacted legislation Baseline for comparison, continuing provisions
4 DoD Comptroller / GAO / CRS Context, analysis, background (not binding)

Quality Control

Source Reliability Ratings

Use this matrix to assess source credibility and determine appropriate citation confidence.

Reliability Matrix

Rating Source Type Appropriate Use Citation Label
AUTHORITATIVE Enacted law, JES, Public Law text Final amounts, binding directives FACT (ENACTED)
HIGH Committee reports, CRS products Intent, analysis, background FACT (REPORT)
MEDIUM Press releases, news coverage Breaking developments, context INFERENCE (VERIFY)
LOW Industry blogs, social media Leads only; verify elsewhere UNKNOWN (UNVERIFIED)
Timing

Update Frequency Calendar

When to check which sources for the most current information.

Check Frequency Guide

Frequency What to Check Why
DAILY Congress.gov bill status, Committee websites, Defense news sites Markups, floor votes, breaking developments during active session
WEEKLY GovTrack, CRFB tracker, SAM.gov opportunities Status changes, new solicitations, amended deadlines
MONTHLY CRS reports, GAO reports, CDMRP funding page Updated analyses, new findings, solicitation cycles
QUARTERLY DoD Comptroller, USASpending, FPDS-NG Obligation data, spending trends, contract awards
ANNUALLY Budget justification documents, CDMRP history New fiscal year baseline, historical comparison

Key Annual Timeline (Typical Appropriations Cycle)

February: President's Budget Request released

March-May: Committee hearings and markups

June-July: House/Senate floor action

September: Conference negotiations (if not under CR)

October 1: New fiscal year begins

Critical Reminders

  • Authorization ≠ Appropriation - NDAA dollars are not spending authority
  • DHP = Title VI - Defense Health Program is in "Other DoD Programs"
  • Always cite - Document + Page + Section + Link
  • Check for incorporation - JES may adopt House/Senate provisions
