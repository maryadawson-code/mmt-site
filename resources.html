Analysis Methodology

The principles and rules that make appropriations analysis accurate and actionable. These aren't just prompts‚Äîthey're a framework for rigorous open-source intelligence.

üìã Core Principles

Open Records First: Start with authoritative public sources‚Äîdon't rely on summaries or secondary analysis. Go to the source.

Citation Mandatory: Every extracted item must include document name, page number, section heading (if present), and link. No exceptions.

Evidence Labeling: Mark everything as:

  • FACT ‚Äî Explicitly stated in primary text/table
  • INFERENCE ‚Äî Reasonable conclusion from context
  • UNKNOWN ‚Äî Cannot be determined from available sources

No Recommendation Without Evidence: Every recommendation must cite the driver item(s). If evidence is incomplete, the recommendation must be conditional and name what's missing.

üìö Source Hierarchy

Priority Order (most authoritative first):

  • 1. Enacted/Conference text + Joint Explanatory Statement (JES) ‚Äî This is the law and accompanying intent
  • 2. House/Senate versions + committee reports ‚Äî Use when conference isn't complete
  • 3. Prior-year enacted ‚Äî Baseline for comparison
  • 4. President's Budget (PB) justification ‚Äî Context only, not controlling

Key Sources:

  • Congress.gov ‚Äî Bill text and status
  • GovInfo.gov ‚Äî Official publications
  • House/Senate Appropriations Committee sites ‚Äî Reports and explanatory statements
  • DoD Comptroller ‚Äî Budget justification books (context only)

Provenance Labels: Tag every line as ENACTED, JES, HOUSE, SENATE, or PB (REQUEST).

üí∞ The Two-Step Budget Rule

This is the #1 mistake in appropriations analysis:

  • AUTHORIZATION (NDAA) = Permission/policy ceilings. Says what you're allowed to do and sets maximum amounts.
  • APPROPRIATION (Defense Approps) = Legal authority to spend. This is the actual money.

Critical Rule: Never treat NDAA dollars as spending authority. Any "funding" answer must be appropriations-based unless the user explicitly asks about authorization.

Hollow Authorization: If NDAA authorizes a program but appropriations provide $0 (or materially less), flag it as: HOLLOW AUTHORIZATION ‚Äî authorized, not funded

Tag Every $ Figure: REQUEST (PB) / HOUSE / SENATE / JES / ENACTED

‚öñÔ∏è Directive Strength Classification

REQUIREMENT (STATUTORY) ‚Äî Binding

  • Keywords: "shall," "must," "none of the funds," "provided that," "may not"
  • Source: Bill text (statutory language)
  • Action: Treat as execution blocker if violated

DIRECTIVE ‚Äî Strongly Expected

  • Keywords: "directs"
  • Source: JES or committee report
  • Action: High political risk if ignored; often becomes reporting requirement

INTENT ‚Äî Soft Guidance

  • Keywords: "encourages," "urges," "notes," "expects," "believes"
  • Action: Informational; elevate only if material to strategy

üè• DHP Special Handling (Title VI)

Location: Defense Health Program (DHP) is consolidated in Title VI (Other DoD Programs)

Sub-Accounts:

  • DHP O&M ‚Äî Majority of funding; includes private sector care/TRICARE
  • DHP RDT&E ‚Äî Medical research, often includes CDMRP
  • DHP Procurement ‚Äî Equipment and IT

Synonym Map (what to search for):

  • TRICARE ‚Üí "Private Sector Care," "Managed Care," "Retail Pharmacy"
  • EHR/IT ‚Üí "Information Management," "IM/IT," "MHS GENESIS"
  • Readiness ‚Üí "Consolidated Health Support," "Education and Training"
  • CDMRP ‚Üí "Peer-Reviewed Medical Research," disease/topic tables

üöß Fence & Limitation Detection

Fence Types to Detect:

  • PROHIBITION: "None of the funds may be used for..."
  • WITHHOLD/FENCE: "Not more than X% may be obligated until..."
  • FLOOR: "Of which not less than..."

For Each Fence, Extract:

  • What is blocked or required
  • Condition to unlock (if withhold)
  • Who must act
  • Due date
  • Citation

Where to Look: Title VIII (General Provisions) and within specific account language in Title VI.

‚úÖ QA Checklist & Failure Modes

Must-Pass Checks:

  • No row without citation
  • Units normalized when stated ("in thousands" ‚Üí full dollars)
  • Provenance tags present (ENACTED/JES/HOUSE/SENATE/PB)
  • Directive strength classified
  • Fences extracted from Title VIII/general provisions
  • DHP handled in Title VI (if DHP topics requested)
  • Unknowns logged with "verify next" steps

Common Failure Modes:

  • Treating NDAA dollars as appropriations
  • Mixing House/Senate report language as final
  • Missing incorporation-by-reference adoption
  • Misreading table units
  • Silent OCR failure (false confidence)
  • Over-inference without explicit report language support

Quick Reference Glossary

Key terms and frameworks from my newsletter analysis. Bookmark this page for quick lookups.

The MHS Triad (2026)

  • Keith Bass (ASD Health Affairs) The political architect. Governance, cost control, telehealth scale-up. GlobalMed background.
  • Emil Michael (USD R&E) The Silicon Valley disruptor. GenAI.mil rollout, China decoupling, university funding fights.
  • Admiral Darin Via (DHA Director) The operational commander. "Human Weapon System" doctrine, Role 2E, HPO infrastructure.

Key Concepts

  • Human Weapon System Via's doctrine treating warfighters as platforms to be optimized, not patients to be treated.
  • Role 2E Expeditionary Medical Unit. Light, mobile surgical teams on high-speed vessels for 96-hour casualty hold.
  • H2F (Holistic Health & Fitness) Army program: 35% fewer injuries, 37% fewer suicides. 20-22 embedded contractors per brigade.
  • Golden Dome Act FY2026 NDAA provisions for layered homeland missile defense. Squeezes O&M where healthcare lives.

Appropriations Terms

  • DHP (Defense Health Program) Title VI consolidated health funding. ~$40B annually for MHS operations.
  • JES (Joint Explanatory Statement) Conference report language explaining congressional intent. More authoritative than committee reports.
  • CDMRP Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs. Peer-reviewed research by disease topic.
  • Hollow Authorization When NDAA authorizes but appropriations provide $0. Authorized ‚â† funded.

The Filter Question

  • "Does this save lives or enhance readiness?" The question the Triad asks about every initiative. If you can't answer it in one sentence, you're going to have a hard time in 2026.
  • Three Magic Words Governance, Speed, Survivability. What Bass, Michael, and Via need from contractors.
  • Audit-Ready Data Architecture Why the Oura deal died. Table stakes for any wearables/RPM pitch to DHA.

Telehealth Landscape

  • Amwell/Leidos ($180M) Enterprise flagship. MHS GENESIS integrated. Contract uncertainty beyond July 2026.
  • GlobalMed Med COI ATO across 721 MTFs. Hardware-integrated clinical telehealth. Bass's former employer.
  • Medweb ($52.8M) Deployed mission: PACS/tele-radiology on 40+ Navy vessels. Critical for Role 2E.

Budget Context (FY2026)

  • $40.5B DHP Request Defense Health Program. 0.2% increase over FY2025.
  • $50B Rural Health Transformation One Big Beautiful Bill Act (July 2025). Symbiotic technology priorities with MHS.
  • 2028 Clean Audit Mandate DoD must achieve clean audit opinion. Section 1002: SES firing authority for failures.
